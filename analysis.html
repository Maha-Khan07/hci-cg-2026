Norman vs. GOMS + KLM Comparison Table

Comparison of Norman’s stages and GOMS + KLM using Splitwise app
Norman’s Stage Real-World Example (Splitwise App) GOMS Interpretation & KLM Operators Alignment Analysis
Goal ↔ GOAL Split the bill fairly among friends so no one feels betrayed or treated unjustly. ✅ Goal: SPLIT-BILL-FAIRLY
Top-level objective in GOMS; defines task hierarchy. 		✅ Direct Match – Both models start from the user’s main intention.
Plan ↔ METHOD & Selection Rules Decide approach in app: Equal split, split by item, or split by custom amounts. ✅ Method & Selection Rules:
• METHOD 1: Equal split
• METHOD 2: By item or custom amounts
• SELECTION RULE: Use equal split if items are simple; otherwise detailed method.

KLM operators (example): [M] think → [P] point to split option → [K] tap → [M] decide → [K] confirm 		✅ Conceptual Match – Norman’s “Plan” corresponds to GOMS method + selection rules; KLM shows step-level actions.
Specify Identify friends in group, assign amounts or check previous balances before confirming. Not explicitly modeled in GOMS.
Mental work to identify correct people/items is abstracted.
KLM: [M] mental prep for calculating who owes what. 		Key Divergence – Norman emphasizes cognitive specification; GOMS assumes operator is known.
Execute ↔ OPERATOR Input amounts, select friends, tap “Split” or “Send” button. Operators:
• Select friends [P][K]
• Enter amounts [K][K]
• Tap confirm/send [P][K]
KLM captures timing for each tap, point, or button press. 		✅ Direct Match – Physical actions in app are primitive GOMS operators.
Perceive / Interpret / Evaluate Verify that the app shows correct balances, friends received notifications, no errors. Not modeled in GOMS.
KLM does not capture cognitive evaluation; GOMS assumes perfect execution. 		Fundamental Divergence – Norman models feedback and awareness; GOMS abstracts it.
